## Core Idea

Binary search works whenever the answer space has a monotonic structure. You keep a left and right boundary, inspect the middle, and discard the half that can no longer contain the answer.

## When To Reach For It

- The input is sorted, or can be interpreted as sorted by some condition.
- You need the first valid index, last valid index, lower bound, or upper bound.
- The problem can be reframed as "find the smallest value that satisfies a predicate."

## What To Watch

- Decide early whether your interval is closed `[left, right]` or half-open `[left, right)`.
- For "first true" style problems, keep the condition and boundary updates perfectly symmetric.
- Off-by-one mistakes usually come from mixing interval conventions.
